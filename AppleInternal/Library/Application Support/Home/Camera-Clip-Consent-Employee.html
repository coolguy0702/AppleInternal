VIDEO COLLECTION: CONSENT AND RELEASE 

Project Name: HomeKit Video Analysis Study


This is a voluntary data collection undertaken by Apple Inc. Your decision to participate is completely voluntary and you may decline to participate or stop your participation at any time. You are not required to participate as a condition of your employment and will not be penalized if you choose not to participate or if you stop your participation. 


By accepting the terms in this Consent and Release Agreement (the "Consent"), you grant to Apple Inc. and all companies within the Apple Inc. group of companies (together referred to as "Apple") the right to collect, store and use your image and voice as described in this Consent. 


WHY IS THIS COLLECTION BEING DONE? 

The HomeKit team is seeking video clips in various settings to improve its camera software. 


HOW DO I PARTICIPATE? 

- You will receive an email from the Project Lead, inviting you to participate in a long-term user study in which you will be asked to submit videos from a HomeKit-supported camera. 

- You will be asked to submit videos from the device in which objects in motion have been detected in a scene, for example: 

1) trees blowing in the wind 

2) pets 

3) a person walking up to a front door 

- You must obtain written consents from all residents of the home and regular visitors, such as significant others, extended family members, and neighbors. 

- To record non-regular guests who are 18+ years old, you must notify them of the camera and Live On program in accordance with the instructions in Attachment 1. 

- To record any individual under 18 years old, you must obtain written consent from the individual's parent/ guardian. 

- You may NOT upload videos that contain any person who has not been notified of the recording as instructed in Attachment 1, any person under the age of 18 without written consent from a parent/ guardian, confidential Apple information, or other sensitive information such as people engaging in private conversations or activities that they would not want submitted to Apple. You must review all videos before uploading to check for prohibited content. 

- The video files you upload may contain metadata that identifies the camera used to capture video, including the camera model, manufacturer, and serial number, as well as the time at which the video was recorded. Either yourself, or a researcher, will tag the video with indications of which portions of the clip contain significant motion, and the type of significant motion (e.g., person, pet, car). 

- The videos you donate will be securely transmitted and stored in a secure storage. Access to this secure storage is limited. Additional details about how to submit a video and the steps to take will be made available. 


WHERE WILL THIS STUDY TAKE PLACE? 

- In your home where your camera is set up. 


WHAT DATA WILL BE COLLECTED? 

The information collected from you may include the following (and are referred to as "Project Data"): 

- Contact Information: Name, Email Address. 

- Video Data: Video, including footage, of 'people in motion.' 

- Device Data: Information related to the camera used, including model and manufacturer and serial number. 


HOW WILL DATA BE USED, DISCLOSED & DESTROYED? 

By signing this Consent, you agree to allow Apple and anyone acting under its control, to collect, use, store, process, and disclose ("use and disclose") the Project Data, for the development, design, approval, production or support of Apple products, technologies, and services subject to the limitations set forth in this Consent. The Project Data will be used, disclosed, and destroyed as follows:

- Contact Information: Your Contact Information generally only be used by limited members of the Project Teams for administrative purposes. 

- Video Data: Your Video Data will be used by Project Teams within Apple with Apple need-to-know business reasons. Your Video Data may additionally be shared with third party vendors or partners that Apple may choose to engage under appropriate contractual obligations. - Device Data: When associated with your Contact Information, your Device Data will generally only be used by a limited number of authorized study personnel working on this Study or subsequent related studies. 

- Data Retention: Your personally identifying Project Data, including Video Data, will be deleted within 10 years after the start date of this study. However, Video Data that has been obscured or blurred may continue to be retained. 


All Project Data may also be provided to public, regulatory, or government authorities, including law enforcement, when required by law, legal process, or litigation. 


You may contact the Project Lead at any time to request deletion of your Project Data, however, note that in some circumstances deletion of your data may not be feasible.
